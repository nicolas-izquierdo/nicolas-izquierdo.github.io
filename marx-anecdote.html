Marx once remarked that his favorite painting was Worker’s Delegation Before the Magistrate by Johann Peter Hasenclever, a German painter of the Düsseldorf school. The painting depicts workers presenting their grievances before the council in Nuremberg. This anecdote has often been cited as a reflection of Marx’s sympathy for the struggles of labor.
