This page collects materials I find useful or inspiring for research in labor politics.

<div class="resources-section">Curated Resource Collections</div>

<div class="resource-item">
**APSA Labor Politics – Teaching Resource Collection** —  
[Link](https://educate.apsanet.org/labor-politics-teaching-resource-collection)  
A curated set of syllabi, podcasts, and teaching materials compiled by APSA’s Labor Politics Group.
</div>

<div class="resource-item">
**Rutgers Labor Studies Research Guide** —  
[Link](https://libguides.rutgers.edu/c.php?g=336652&p=2266813)  
A research guide offering key books, databases, and archival resources in labor studies.
</div>

<div class="resource-item">
**Cornell ILR Labor & Employment Research Guide** —  
[Link](https://guides.library.cornell.edu/c.php?g=637571&p=4462748)  
Comprehensive navigation through ILR’s labor research tools, including journals, datasets, and policy sources.
</div>

<div class="resource-item">
**UnionStats.com (Hirsch & Macpherson)** —  
[Link](https://unionstats.com/)  
The standard reference for annual U.S. union membership and coverage statistics derived from the CPS.
</div>

<div class="resource-item">
**Cornell ILR – Statistical Sources: Labor & Union Data** —  
[Link](https://guides.library.cornell.edu/StatisticalSources/laborunionstats)  
A gateway to datasets and statistical repositories on labor markets, unions, and collective bargaining.
</div>
