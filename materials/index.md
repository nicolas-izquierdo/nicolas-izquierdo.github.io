This page collects materials I find useful or inspiring for research in labor politics.

---

<div class="resource-card">
<h3>APSA Labor Politics – Teaching Resource Collection</h3>
<p><a href="https://educate.apsanet.org/labor-politics-teaching-resource-collection">https://educate.apsanet.org/labor-politics-teaching-resource-collection</a></p>
<p>A curated set of syllabi, podcasts, and teaching materials compiled by APSA’s Labor Politics Group.</p>
</div>

<div class="resource-card">
<h3>Rutgers U.S. Labor and Working Class History Guide</h3>
<p><a href="https://libguides.rutgers.edu/c.php?g=336652&p=2266813">https://libguides.rutgers.edu/c.php?g=336652&p=2266813</a></p>
<p>A research guide highlighting key books, databases, and archival collections in U.S. labor and working-class history.</p>
</div>

<div class="resource-card">
<h3>Cornell ILR – Labor & Employment Research Guide</h3>
<p><a href="https://guides.library.cornell.edu/c.php?g=637571&p=4462748">https://guides.library.cornell.edu/c.php?g=637571&p=4462748</a></p>
<p>A comprehensive overview of ILR’s labor research tools, including journals, datasets, and policy resources.</p>
</div>

<div class="resource-card">
<h3>UnionStats.com (Hirsch, Macpherson and Even)</h3>
<p><a href="https://unionstats.com/">https://unionstats.com/</a></p>
<p>The standard reference for yearly U.S. union membership and coverage statistics derived from the CPS.</p>
</div>

<div class="resource-card">
<h3>Cornell ILR – Statistical Sources: Labor & Union Data</h3>
<p><a href="https://guides.library.cornell.edu/StatisticalSources/laborunionstats">https://guides.library.cornell.edu/StatisticalSources/laborunionstats</a></p>
<p>A gateway to datasets and statistical repositories on labor markets, unions, and collective bargaining.</p>
</div>
